## Schema Design Decisions

### Why SERIAL for users, UUID for events
Users are created sequentially and referenced by small integer FKs throughout. Events are generated by distributed workers and must never collide — UUID with `gen_random_uuid()` solves this without coordination.

### Why JSONB for payload columns
`agent_events.payload` and `agent_runs.output` store polymorphic data (mention events have different shape than trend events). JSONB gives flexibility without schema proliferation. Filterable fields (source, type, status) stay as proper columns with indexes.

### Why status enums over booleans
All stateful entities use explicit status enums: `user.status`, `agent_events.status`, `trend.status`, `alert_deliveries.status`. This enables audit queries ("show me all failed events today") and supports future workflow states without adding columns.

### Why trendSources separate from trends
Trends are aggregated summaries. TrendSources are individual evidence records. Separating them allows source-level trust scoring and prevents trends from being inflated by a single high-engagement cast counted multiple times.
